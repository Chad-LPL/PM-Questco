## Product intent

Taylor on **Azure** (dev + production at minimum): deploy, observe, secure, CI/CD—closes [2.1.1](./2.1.1-Review-and-Define-Technical-Architecture.md). Questco provisions **separate resource groups** for dev and production; LPL receives **full control within those RGs** (self-contained builds). **CI/CD** leans on **GitHub** (Questco repo) and **GitHub Actions** rather than a separate third-party platform; Questco SSO applies to repo access. Compute should favor **serverless and low-ops** options (Azure Functions, Web Apps for Functions-oriented workloads); **containers/Kubernetes (e.g. AKS)** only when needed; **avoid raw VMs** unless justified. Foundation for [2.2.1](./2.2.1-Prism-HR-API-Setup-authentication-connectivity-normalization.md)–[2.2.3](./2.2.3-SharePoint-file-storage-configuration.md).

## Product requirements (verifiable)

- **Environments:** Dev and prod **resource groups** (Questco naming/ownership); parity for config/secrets. Key Vault for integrations; who accesses what (LPL/Questco).
- **CI/CD:** Pipelines via **GitHub Actions** to non-prod (and prod per policy); document branch/deploy flow with Questco.
- **Compute:** Written choice + rationale aligned with Questco: **serverless-first**; collaborate with Questco IT on borderline choices (e.g. containerized vs Web Apps).
- **Cost & governance:** Surface **forecast** for new spend; **escalate** material recurring cost (Questco cited **~$500/mo** as a threshold for explicit discussion). Architecture **review with Questco IT** (e.g. Chris/Jagger) before large or expensive designs land.
- Observability + health endpoint. Outbound to Prism, ClientSpace, Graph tested.
- Deploy/prod-data access documented.
